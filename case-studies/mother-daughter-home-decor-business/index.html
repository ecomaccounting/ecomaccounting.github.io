Mother-Daughter Home Decor Business

Partnership to Private Limited Conversion

Client Profile

  • Business: Handcrafted home decor items
  • Founders: Mother (55) and daughter (28)
  • Current Structure: Partnership firm
  • Stage: 2 years old, ₹1.2Cr revenue

The Challenge

Sunita and her daughter Ananya had been running their home decor business successfully for 2 years as a partnership firm. They sold on Amazon, Flipkart, and their own website. Now they faced limitations:

  • Partnership structure limiting growth potential
  • Unable to raise funding (investors wanted Pvt Ltd structure)
  • Personal liability concerns as business scaled
  • High personal tax burden (no corporate tax benefits)
  • Succession planning concerns (Sunita wanted clarity for family)

"We started small, so partnership made sense. But now with ₹1.2 crore revenue and plans to raise ₹50 lakhs, banks and investors won't take us seriously."

Our Solution

Phase 1: Structure Analysis & Planning (Week 1)

Evaluated options:

  1. Continue as partnership: ❌ Limited fundraising, unlimited liability
  2. Convert to LLP: ⚠️ Better liability protection but still funding limitations
  3. Form new Private Limited: ✅ Best for fundraising, limited liability, professional image

Recommended approach: Form new Pvt Ltd company, transfer business from partnership

Phase 2: Incorporation & Transfer (Week 2-4)

Step 1: New Company Formation

  • Incorporated
  • Share capital: ₹10 lakhs (1,00,000 shares at ₹10 each)
  • Shareholding: Sunita 60%, Ananya 40%
  • Directors: Both appointed

Step 2: Business Transfer

  • Slump sale agreement from partnership to company
  • Transferred all assets:
    • Inventory: ₹18 lakhs
    • Furniture & fixtures: ₹3 lakhs
    • Brand name, customer database, IP
  • Assumed all liabilities:
    • Vendor payables: ₹8 lakhs
    • Working capital loan: ₹5 lakhs

Step 3: Regulatory Transfers

  • Transferred GST registration to new entity
  • Updated all marketplace seller accounts (Amazon, Flipkart)
  • Changed bank account signatories
  • Updated vendor and customer contracts
  • Professional Tax, TDS, and other licenses updated

Step 4: Closure of Partnership

  • Filed for partnership firm dissolution
  • Cleared all partnership liabilities
  • Final accounts and tax returns filed
  • No tax incidence due to slump sale treatment

Phase 3: Post-Conversion Setup (Week 5-6)

Corporate Governance

  • Board meeting structure established (quarterly)
  • Clear role division:
    • Sunita: MD, product development, artisan relations
    • Ananya: Director, marketing, eCommerce operations
  • Hired virtual CFO services for financial planning

Fundraising Preparation

  • Clean cap table created
  • Shareholders agreement drafted
  • Prepared for potential investor round
  • Financial statements restated in company format

Legal & IP Protection

  • Trademark application for brand name
  • Employment contracts for 8 team members
  • Vendor agreements standardized

The Results

Immediate Outcomes:

✅ Pvt Ltd company operational in 6 weeks
✅ All business transferred seamlessly (zero customer impact)
✅ Professional structure attractive to investors
✅ Limited liability protection for personal assets

Business Impact (12 months post-conversion):

** Fundraising Success:**

  • Raised ₹50 lakhs from angel investors (3 months after conversion)
  • Valuation: ₹3.5 Cr pre-money
  • Investors specifically cited professional structure as confidence factor

Tax Efficiency:

  • Partnership tax burden: 30% + surcharge on Sunita's income
  • Company tax: 25% + lower dividend tax for shareholders

Growth Acceleration:

  • Revenue grew to ₹2.8 Cr (133% growth)
  • Expanded to 120+ SKUs from 45
  • Launched Amazon Global (export) program
  • Hired professional CEO (Ananya moved to CMO role)

Succession Clarity:

  • Clear ownership structure protects family
  • Ananya's path to majority ownership defined
  • Professional board established for governance

Cost-Benefit Analysis:

Investment in Conversion:

  • Professional fees: ₹75,000
  • Stamp duty & registration: ₹25,000
  • Total: ₹1,00,000

Returns in Year 1:

  • Tax savings: ₹3.5 lakhs
  • Fundraising enabled: ₹50 lakhs
  • Credibility with suppliers: Better payment terms
  • ROI: 350%+ in year one
